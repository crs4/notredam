NotreDAM

The open-source Digital Asset Management platform

 
 

An overview of NotreDAM

 

In a society more and more based on knowledge and on dematerialisation of goods, digital assets play a key role and it is therefore essential to organize them in an efficient way. The growth of digital content industries has raised the need for solutions capable of managing large amounts of assets across their whole lifecycle.

The term Digital Asset Management (DAM) identifies tools and methodologies for ingesting, describing, classifying, archiving and adapting for distribution the digital assets of an organization. However, it is important to highlight that the benefits of Digital Asset Management are not circumscribed to organizations. The spread of user-generated content (just think how easy is to produce in a short time, with a digital camera, thousands of images) has extended these benefits also to individual users and to informal groups of enthusiasts. An analysis of ABI Research (May 2008) forecasts that the world market for Digital Asset Management is expected to reach one billion dollars in 2008, from 330 millions of 2007.

NotreDAM is a Digital Asset Management application jointly developed by CRS4 and by the Digital Content Lab of Sardegna Ricerche.

The main features of NotreDAM are described hereafter.

  1. ‣NotreDAM is a collaborative tool


  1. The target users of NotreDAM are people involved in content production, archiving and publishing, that collaborate remotely sharing content and methodologies.

  2. The concept of "workspace", intended as a working environment accessible to a group of users, is central in NotreDAM. The workspace is the context in which digital assets are used in order to achieve a given goal. Within a workspace, a group can customize its own workflow and define collections and taxonomies for classifying content.

  3. The workspace also provides tools to facilitate the communication among users and to highlight specific events.


  1. ‣NotreDAM can manage multimedia files


  1. NotreDAM can manage several types of objects, such as images, audio, video and textual documents, and supports the most common encoding formats.

  2. Users can define different views of an object for different usage contexts. These views are fully customizable and are automatically built adapting the original object with operations such as transcoding, preview extraction, resizing, resampling, watermarking, etc.. In this way it is possible to define, for instance, a "public view" of an image, with a maximum given width and a Creative Commons license, a "customer view" with full resolution and a copyright watermark, and so on.


  1. ‣NotreDAM supports open metadata standards


  1. Adobe XMP (Extensible Metadata Platform) is an open specification that encompasses several metadata schemas. XMP allows to embed metadata into resource files, in order to ensure interoperability among applications and reusability of content.


  3. NotreDAM adopts XMP for content description and supports other metadata standards (EXIF, IPTC, etc.) as well. Custom metadata schemes can be easily added as XMP extensions.


  5. ‣NotreDAM supports geo-referencing of content


  1. NotreDAM features an intuitive interface (based on Google Maps) that allows to associate geographical coordinates to objects through simple drag & drop actions and to perform search operations by selecting an area on a map.


  1. ‣NotreDAM is accessible through the web


  1. NotreDAM is totally web-based and can be accessed through most web browsers. No installation or periodical updates are required on the client-side and users are not contrained to use a particular operating system.


  1. ‣NotreDAM is open to user communities


  1. Although NotreDAM objects are accessible, as a rule, only to authorized users, any object can be put in a public area by its owner. External users can browse the public area of NotreDAM, bookmark interesting objects and, in case, add description tags and ratings. These tags and ratings build up a "bottom-up" classification that can be exploited to improve the description of the objects.


  1. ‣NotreDAM is based on a distributed and scalable architecture


  1. NotreDAM is based on MediaDART, an open-source framework based on a p2p network of PCs (nodes) that act both as a distributed storage and as a concurrent resource processor. New nodes can be added at any time to the MediaDART network in order to increase storage and processing power.


  1. ‣NotreDAM is open-source


  1. NotreDAM is distributed under the GNU Public License (GPL) v.3, and can be installed on any server with GNU/Linux Ubuntu 9.10.
